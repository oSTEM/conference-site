</div>

</div>

The panels will feature diverse voices and perspectives on the following themes:

                <td className="pr-4 align-top font-bold text-nav-blue">Medical Students Across the Spectrum of Healing</td>
                <td>This panel explores the diverse experiences of medical students who bring their full identities into healthcare training. Panelists will discuss navigating medical, veterinary, dental, nursing, pharmacy, etc... school while holding intersecting identities, challenges within clinical environments, and the importance of representation in patient care. The conversation will highlight how personal identity can shape approaches to healing, empathy, and advocacy in medicine, as well as strategies for building supportive communities within high-pressure academic and clinical spaces.</td>
            </tr>
            <tr>
                <td className="pr-4 align-top font-bold text-nav-blue">Mentorship and Community in STEM</td>
                <td>This panel focuses on how mentorship and peer networks shape success and belonging in STEM fields. Panelists will discuss how to find mentors across disciplines and identities, the value of peer mentorship, and ways to build community when representation is limited. The conversation will also explore how individuals can create their own support systems, pay mentorship forward, and foster inclusive environments that help others thrive academically and professionally.</td>
            </tr>
            <tr>
                <td className="pr-4 align-top font-bold text-nav-blue">Beyond the Binary: Intersectionality in STEM Spaces</td>
                <td>This panel examines how intersecting identities, such as race, gender, sexuality, disability, and socioeconomic background, shape experiences in STEM. Panelists will discuss barriers to inclusion, the limitations of one-dimensional diversity efforts, and the importance of recognizing complexity in identity. The conversation will highlight strategies for creating more equitable STEM environments where individuals are not forced to compartmentalize who they are in order to belong or succeed.</td>
            </tr>
            <tr>
                <td className="pr-4 align-top font-bold text-nav-blue">Leading with Purpose, Advocating with Pride</td>
                <td>This panel explores how individuals in STEM can step into leadership roles while staying grounded in advocacy and authenticity. Panelists will discuss building influence within academic, industry, and research spaces, using their voices to drive change, and navigating leadership as members of marginalized communities. The conversation will highlight strategies for effective advocacy, mentorship through leadership, and sustaining purpose while pushing for more inclusive and equitable STEM environments.</td>
            </tr>
            <tr>
                <td className="pr-4 align-top font-bold text-nav-blue">Queer Experiences in STEM Across the World</td>
                <td>This panel highlights the diverse experiences of LGBTQ+ individuals working and studying in STEM across different cultural, institutional, and geographic contexts. Panelists will share stories of navigating identity, visibility, and belonging in global STEM spaces, as well as the challenges and progress seen in different regions. The discussion will explore how culture and policy shape lived experiences, while also emphasizing shared themes of resilience, community, and connection across borders.</td>
            </tr>
